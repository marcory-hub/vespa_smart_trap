The invasive Vespa velutina can be contaminated by indigenous isolates of Beauveria bassiana and Metarhizium robertsii.

- Overexpression of proteases (e.g., PR1 protease in Metarhizium, chitinase in Beauveria) can accelerate insect lethality, and secondary metabolites or toxins (e.g., destruxins) further disrupt vital systems.[](https://www.scielo.br/j/aib/a/7RCCHwZ4YhQWpdVVZfJ6LXG/?lang=en)​

- Protozoan and viral co-infections are common, but their utility as biocontrol agents against hornets is less direct than fungi.[](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-90615-7)​
